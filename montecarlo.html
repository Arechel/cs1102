What is Monte Carlo?

Monte Carlo is a method that follows a random approach and does not follow time.
It generates a multitude of configurations by proposing random moves and accepting
or rejecting them according to the Metropolis criterion which is based on energy differences and temperature.
Monte Carlo is efficient in calculating properties of equilibriums, free energy, and different phases.
