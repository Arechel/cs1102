What is Computational Chemistry?

Computational chemistry is an area in chemistry
that utilizes advanced mathematical algorithms to render and predict the properties and behaviors of molecules and different systems.
Using computer science, physics, and mathematics,
Computational chemistry allows us to solve chemical problems digitally instead of spending time recreating laboratory experiments and testing different solutions.

Why is Computational Chemistry Needed?

Computation chemistry is a necessity due to the many chemical problems we cannot solve efficiently, cost-effectively, or safely.
Physical experiments tend to be extremely expensive, time-consuming, and sometimes impossible.
Examples of this are highly radioactive, toxic, or potentially explosive chemicals that we would need to research.
Different computational methods are required to overcome these limitations and allow scientists to fully map molecules and predict structures at a microscopic level on the computer.
This would allow for faster drug discoveries, deeper scientific research, and quicker results that could avoid disasters at a much higher rate such as Covid-19.
