What are Force Fields?


A force field is a mathematical equation that descrives the potential energy of a molecular system through its atomic position.
It approximates the real quantum-mechanical energy landscape using classical mechanics, allowing simulations to run quickly even on millions of atoms.

Both Molecular Dynamics and Monte Carlo depend heavily on the accuracy of different force fields.
The quality of force fields affects whether the simulation matches reality.Example of force fields include AMBER, CHARMM and OPLS

Force Field Primary Use Key Features
AMBER Proteins, DNA All-atom, transferable parameters
CHARMM Biomolecules, small molecules Includes polarizable variants
OPLS Liquids, organics Optimized for simulations
GROMOS Biomolecules in solution United-atom options
ReaxFF Reactive systems (batteries, combustion) Bond breaking/formation, variable connectivity
