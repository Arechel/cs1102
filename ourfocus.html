Our Focus

Numerical simulations such as Molecular Dynamics and Monte Carlo
make it possible to study the complex behavior of molecules over time or at equilibrium.
This would help greatly reduce costs, fully accelerate innovation in chemistry and explore greater things.



Numerical Simulations

Numerical Simulations are powerful computer-based methods that enable scientists
to study molecules without needing laboratory equipment.
These simulations help bridge the gap between different theoretical models and
real-world laboratory experiments by simulating how the molecules move, interact, bond,
and change over time under any conditions we can put them through.
At its core, numerical simulations rely fully on classical mechanics and statistical physics
to model the complex systems that would be too large or too fast for full mechanical calculations.
Instead of solving the Schrödinger equation for every electron,
simulations use simplified mathematical models called force fields.
