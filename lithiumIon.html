Lithium-Ion Batteries

The production of lithium-ion batteries utilized Molecular Dynamic simulations to study the formation of the Solid Electrolyte Interphase (SEI) layer on graphite on anodes.
The SEI is a protective film that is formed during the first charging cycle of the battery.
It is an essential part of the battery's safety and long-term consistent performance. These simulations are required
due to its atomic-level formation mechanisms being extremely difficult to observe directly in experiments.
Scientists use specific reactive force fields (eg. ReaxFF) in molecular dynamic simulations to model the decomposition of electrolyte solvents at the anode surface,
the effects of electrolyte properties and SEI growth kinetics. This is done all before any of the physical batteries are built.
The testing has allowed the design of batteries to be more stable and have safer high-energy-density batteries.
Monte Carlo methods are sometimes combined with molecular dynamics in this area on rare events that may happen during its decomposition of the SEI formation.

The model used in the SEI formation is the ReaxFF reactive force field.
This model was used for molecular dynamics simulation to simulate bond breaking and formations during the decomposition of parts of the electrolytes.
